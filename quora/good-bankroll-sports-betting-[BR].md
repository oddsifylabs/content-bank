# What is a good bankroll for sports betting? [BR]

**Answer:**

The right bankroll depends on your bet sizing discipline, not a magic number. Here's the framework:

## The 1% Rule (Non-Negotiable)

Professional bettors risk **1% of bankroll per bet**, maximum. This means:

| Bankroll | 1 Unit (1%) | Max Bet (1.5u) |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| $500 | $5 | $7.50 |
| $1,000 | $10 | $15 |
| $5,000 | $50 | $75 |
| $10,000 | $100 | $150 |

**Why 1%?** Even with a 55% win rate (excellent), you'll have 10-20% drawdowns. At 1% sizing, a 20% bankroll loss = 20 losing units. Painful but recoverable.

At 5% sizing? That same drawdown = 100% bankroll loss. Game over.

## Minimum Starting Bankroll

**Realistic minimum: $500-1,000**

Here's why:
- At $100 bankroll, 1 unit = $1
- Most books have $5-10 minimum bets
- You're forced to risk 5-10% per bet → guaranteed ruin

**Sweet spot: $2,000-5,000**
- 1 unit = $20-50
- Meaningful returns without excessive risk
- Can absorb 20-30 unit downswings

## The Honest Math

Path from $1,000 → $10,000:
- **500-800 bets minimum** (18-24 months at 1 bet/day)
- **55% win rate** at -110 odds = ~5% ROI
- **Survive 3-5 drawdowns** of 10-20%
- **Success rate: ~5-8%** of people who start

Why so low? Variance destroys the undisciplined. People:
- Increase bet size after losses (chasing)
- Deviate from process (emotional betting)
- Quit during inevitable downswings

## Bankroll Management Rules

1. **Recalculate unit size every Sunday**
   - New bankroll × 1% = new unit
   - Down $500? Your unit shrinks. That's protection.

2. **Never chase losses with bigger bets**
   - Lost 5 units today? Tomorrow is still 1 unit max.

3. **Separate betting bankroll from life money**
   - This is risk capital. If you can't afford to lose it, don't bet it.

4. **Track every bet in a spreadsheet**
   - Date, sport, bet type, odds, stake, result, CLV
   - Review monthly: are you actually profitable or just lucky?

## When to Increase Bankroll

Only add money if:
- You've tracked 100+ bets with positive CLV
- Your process is disciplined (no emotional deviations)
- You can afford the addition without financial stress

**Don't** add money to cover losses. That's throwing good money after bad.
