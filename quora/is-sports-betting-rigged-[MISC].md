# Is sports betting rigged? [MISC]

**Answer:**

No, but the **structure is designed for you to lose** — that's different from rigging.

## What's NOT Rigged

- **Game outcomes** — NFL, NBA, MLB games aren't fixed for betting
- **Player props** — Players aren't told to go over/under
- **Lines moving** — It's not personal, it's money flow

Books make money on the **vig** (juice), not by screwing your parlay.

## What IS Designed Against You

**1. The Vig (Juice)**
- Standard -110 odds = 52.4% needed to break even
- Fair odds would be -100 (50%)
- **House edge: 4.5% built in**

**2. Parlay Pricing**
- 2-leg parlay true odds: +301
- Books pay: +260
- **House edge: 12%+**

**3. Promotion Traps**
- "Risk-free" bets (not actually risk-free)
- Profit boosts (on -EV lines)
- Parlay insurance (encourages bad bets)

**4. Speed of Play**
- Live betting = faster losses
- One-click bets = less thinking
- Push notifications = more FOMO

## Why It FEELS Rigged

**Variance clustering:**
- You'll have 5-7 loss streaks per 100 bets
- Losses feel worse than wins (psychology)
- You remember bad beats, forget good ones

**Confirmation bias:**
- "My parlay got screwed" (forget 3 that hit)
- "Book moved line after I bet" (forget times it moved your way)
- "Rigged" is easier than "I got unlucky"

## The Sharp Mindset

**Books aren't your enemy — they're the casino.**

Casinos don't rig blackjack. They just have math on their side.

Same with sportsbooks:
- They don't need to rig your bet
- They just need you to:
  - Bet parlays
  - Not line shop
  - Chase losses
  - Ignore CLV

**Do the opposite, and you're a threat to their hold.**
