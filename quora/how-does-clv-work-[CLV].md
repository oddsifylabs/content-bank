# How does closing line value (CLV) work in sports betting? [CLV]

**Answer:**

Closing Line Value (CLV) is the single most important metric in sharp sports betting — more important than win/loss record. Here's why:

## What is CLV?

The **closing line** is the final odds/point spread available before a game starts. CLV measures whether you got better odds than the closing line.

**Example:**
- You bet: Chiefs -3 at -110 (10am Tuesday)
- Closing line: Chiefs -4.5 at -110 (4pm Sunday)
- **You beat the market by 1.5 points**

Even if the Chiefs lose by 2 (your bet loses), you **won the CLV game**. The market moved in your direction, meaning your information/timing was better than the public.

## Why CLV > Win/Loss

Sports betting has massive variance. You can:
- Make a terrible bet and win (luck)
- Make a great bet and lose (variance)

But over 500+ bets, if you consistently beat the closing line, you **will** be profitable. Here's the math:

| Your Bet | Closing Line | Result | CLV Verdict |
|----------|--------------|--------|-------------|
| -3 | -4.5 | Chiefs win by 2 | ❌ Bet loses, ✅ CLV win |
| -3 | -4.5 | Chiefs win by 5 | ✅ Bet wins, ✅ CLV win |
| -3 | -4.5 | Chiefs lose by 1 | ❌ Bet loses, ✅ CLV win |

You only lose the CLV game if the line moves **against** you (e.g., you bet -3, it closes -2.5).

## How to Track CLV

1. **Record your bet** (line, odds, timestamp, sportsbook)
2. **Check closing line** (use Action Network, Covers, or book's line history)
3. **Calculate difference** (your line vs. closing line)
4. **Track over time** (spread bets: points; moneyline: implied probability)

**Positive CLV rate of 60%+** = you're beating the market consistently.
